What is Programming?

Programming

  • The action or process of writing computer programs.
  • Predetermined behavior.

These are really bad definitions, I snagged them from Google's dictionary while trying to find a good way of explaining what programming was. Let's try again shall we?

Programming

  • Programming is writing a program?
  • Programming is writing code?

Well, that's less than useless, maybe let's try another? Nope! still useless. Guess we're not asking the right question.

Why Program Stuff?

  • To make it do something cool/useful
  • Automate tasks

That's Better!

  • Programming - telling a computer how to do something

How Do We Do It

  • It's like writing a recipe

Steps

  • Declare Your Ingredients
  • Follow Directions
  • Clean Up

Grilled Cheese - Ingredients

  • 2 Slices of Bread
  • Butter
  • Cheese
  • Tomato

Normally I don't like tomato on my grilled cheese but for this example I needed to make an exception.

Grilled Cheese - Directions

  1. Slice cheese into thin slices
  2. Slice tomato
  3. Apply butter to bread
  4. Place cheese and tomato on bread
  5. Place all of that in your pan
  6. Heat until cheese is melted

Ok, so we have our recipe. Cool, what happens next?

Ooops

What happened

  • Instructions were ambiguous
  • Instructions were incomplete

How much tomato or cheese, how much butter. How do I place it in the pan? With a person you can count on some common sense but with a computer it follows what you say exactly. Notice how I never said to remove from the pan? Or place the pan on the stove? I also never told it what a pan or a stove was. I didn't say how to heat it. I'm going to skip the cleanup step because I've made my point.

Takeaways

  • Computers are kinda dumb
  • Programs must be explicit
  • Andrew eats a lot of Ramen noodles

Assume that a computer is dense, really really dense. So, how can we be explicit and explain to them completely what to do in a concise manner? Also, I really don't eat Ramen noodles, they're really bad for you... too much sodium.

Programming Languages

  • Typically human readable languages that can 'easily' be converted to machine readable instructions

What do they look like?

int factorial(int x) {
      if (x == 1) 
        return x; 
      else 
        return x * factorial(x - 1);
    }

Well, that looks ugly and confusing doesn't it? Well, what is it actually saying.

Translation

  • There's a function called factorial
  • It maps it's input (an integer that we just named x) to another integer
  • Everything inside the braces is just how it maps it

A brief aside about mathematics

  • This is all math
  • A function is just something that takes inputs and produces an output
  • We can denote this as f(x) => y
  • IMPORTANT if f(1) => 4 it should always map to 4.
